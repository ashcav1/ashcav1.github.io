Research

Internal Mass Distribution of Bennu [under review — Icarus]

The OSIRIS-REx mission provided the first measured gravity field of a sub-kilometer asteroid. This work represents (101955) Bennu as a constant-density shape model with a set of superimposed polyhedral components, and estimates component densities using an iterative, constrained, covariance-weighted least-squares formulation that enforces global mass conservation while reproducing the measured gravity coefficients.

The estimated model produces an under-dense inner core and equatorial torus, balanced by an over-dense subsurface layer, and provides a framework for constructing internal density distributions consistent with the measured gravity field.

Spacecraft–Regolith Interaction [active]

Surface operations at rubble-pile asteroids depend on the mechanical response of loosely bound regolith under very low gravity. This work uses non-smooth Contact Dynamics in LMGC90 with polyhedral particles to simulate spacecraft–surface interaction without the artificial stiffness and damping parameters required by soft-sphere methods.

Touchdown observables such as penetration depth, contact duration, and rebound behavior constrain surface properties including cohesion, packing fraction, and friction.

Binary Asteroid Dynamics [active]

The General Use Binary Asteroid Simulator (GUBAS) computes the mutual gravitational potential, libration, and orbital evolution of binary systems such as Didymos–Dimorphos.

Ongoing work couples GUBAS-derived surface gravity, slope, and descent conditions to LMGC90 surface simulations, so that landing, sampling, and mobility analyses are carried out in a self-consistent binary environment.

About

Ashish Cavale is a Ph.D. student in Aerospace Engineering Sciences at the University of Colorado Boulder, working in the Celestial and Spaceflight Mechanics Laboratory under Prof. Daniel J. Scheeres. His research focuses on the internal structure of rubble-pile asteroids, constrained by OSIRIS-REx gravity measurements, and on granular mechanics simulations of spacecraft–surface interaction in low-gravity environments.
